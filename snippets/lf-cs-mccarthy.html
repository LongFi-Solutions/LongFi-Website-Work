The Challenge

Strong Wi-Fi, but still no indoor cell signal.

The offices had strong wireless, and indoor cellular still fell short, so the older fixes each came up short.

Dead zones indoors

Strong Wi-Fi, and still weak cell coverage in the same rooms.

Logins that expired

Guest portal credentials lapsed on a 30 to 60 day cycle, and again at each site.

A fix that was too big

The distributed antenna system quote came back oversized and overpriced.

Four groups to serve

Home-base staff, travelers between offices, visitors, and contractors.

The Solution

LongFi Connect: a simple improvement that uses the offices' existing Wi-Fi to fix indoor cell coverage.

It went on as a Passpoint configuration on the existing Cisco access points, so phones connect on their SIM and coverage scales office to office.

  • Connects on the SIM. Phones attach on carrier credentials, with no password, no portal, and no app.
  • No new hardware. A configuration on the Cisco network already in operation, not a construction project.
  • Coverage by area. Access points are grouped by area, so specific floors are included or left out.
  • Secure by design. Carrier SIM authentication and encryption, on a dedicated lane apart from business systems.
  • No added cost. Runs on the network in place, and the operator can disable it at any time.
