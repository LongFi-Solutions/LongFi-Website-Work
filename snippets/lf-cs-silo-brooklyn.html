The Challenge

A landmark building that blocked the signal guests rely on.

SILO lives inside a repurposed hangar. The scale and dense construction are part of what makes the venue special, and also what kept outdoor cellular signal from reaching deep inside. On busy nights, guests hit dead spots at the exact moments they needed their phones.

The tab cannot close at the table

One group once walked outside at 3 a.m. just to pay a large tab.

The ride text lands late

Rides and texts stall right when guests are trying to get home.

The night goes unposted

Posts and stories that fill the room next weekend never upload live.

The Solution

LongFi Connect improves indoor cell connectivity using the venue's excess Wi-Fi capacity.

It runs as a configuration on the business-grade Wi-Fi already in the room, so phones connect on their own and keep working when the floor is full.

  • Automatic for guests. Phones connect on their own, with no app, no password, and no portal.
  • Carrier signal over the room's Wi-Fi. Traffic from major mobile carriers is carried indoors across the Wi-Fi already in the building.
  • Secure by design. Guest traffic is authenticated and encrypted, and kept separate from the venue's systems.
  • No cost to the venue. Activation takes minimal I.T. time and runs quietly alongside operations.
  • Built on what is already there. The venue keeps its network and its guest experience.
