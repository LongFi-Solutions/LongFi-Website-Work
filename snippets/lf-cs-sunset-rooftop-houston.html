The Challenge

When the venue fills up, indoor cell service gives out.

The busier a venue gets, the weaker its indoor cell service becomes. With FIFA World Cup 2026™ bringing heavy fan traffic to East Downtown before and after each Houston match, Sunset faced that on its busiest days of the year.

Tickets and payments stall

Mobile tickets and tap-to-pay slow down when the rooftop fills up.

Rides and calls drop

Guests struggle to reach their group or call a ride on a packed match day.

The moment goes unshared

Photos and posts that show off Houston never upload live.

The Solution

LongFi Connect improves indoor cell connectivity using the venue's excess Wi-Fi capacity.

It runs on the business-grade Wi-Fi already at the venue, so phones connect on their own and keep working when the rooftop is full.

  • Automatic, no password. Guests connect on their own, with no password and no app, using Passpoint and the credentials already on the device.
  • Engineered for peak density. Built around how many people are on it and what they are doing when the rooftop is full.
  • Runs alongside operations. Works with the cameras, point-of-sale, and event operations without adding a burden on the staff.
  • Secure by design. Guest traffic is authenticated and encrypted, and kept separate from the venue's systems.
  • Permanent infrastructure. Built to outlast the tournament, it keeps serving guests on busy weekends after it.
